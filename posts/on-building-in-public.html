On Building in Public

2026-03-27

"The best way to predict the future is to create it." — Peter Drucker

There is something powerful about working in the open. When you build in public, you invite accountability, feedback, and serendipity into your process. The work becomes a conversation rather than a monologue.

Why It Matters

Most creators work in isolation until they have something "ready." But readiness is a moving target. The pursuit of perfection becomes the enemy of progress. Building in public flips this — you share the rough edges, the false starts, the learning moments.

What you get in return:

  • Real feedback from real people, early enough to matter
  • A body of documented thinking you can reference later
  • Connections with others who share your interests
  • A forcing function for clarity — if you can't explain it, you don't understand it

The Practice

Building in public doesn't mean streaming every keystroke. It means sharing your thinking at regular intervals. Write about what you're learning. Document your decisions and the reasoning behind them. Show the work, not just the result.

Start small. A single post about something you figured out today is enough. Consistency matters more than volume.

The Risk

Yes, someone might copy your idea. But ideas are cheap — execution is everything. The person who builds in public compounds their learning and their network. The person who hides their work compounds only their anxiety.

Ship it. Share it. See what happens.
