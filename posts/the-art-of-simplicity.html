The Art of Simplicity

2026-03-20

"Perfection is achieved, not when there is nothing more to add, but when there is nothing left to take away." — Antoine de Saint-Exupéry

We are drawn to complexity. More features, more options, more everything. But the things that endure — the tools we reach for daily, the writing we return to, the designs that feel effortless — are almost always simple.

Simplicity Is Not Easy

Simple is hard. It requires you to understand a problem deeply enough to distill it to its essence. Anyone can add. The skill is in knowing what to remove.

Consider the best tools you use. They do one thing well. They don't ask you to configure twelve settings before you start. They respect your time and your attention.

Applied to Writing

The same principle applies to writing. Every word should earn its place. If a sentence doesn't move the reader forward, cut it. If a paragraph exists only to make the author feel smart, cut it harder.

Good writing is:

  • Clear over clever
  • Concise over comprehensive
  • Honest over impressive

Applied to Building

When you're building something — a product, a project, a habit — start with the smallest version that could work. Not a prototype. Not an MVP. The smallest thing. Ship that. Learn from it. Then decide what to add.

Most of the time, you'll find that the small version was enough.

A Reminder

Complexity is the default. Simplicity is the craft. Choose the harder path — your future self will thank you.
